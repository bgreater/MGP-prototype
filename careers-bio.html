Ashley Gill, former Business Intern currently serving as Communications Specialist

What are the job responsibilities you perform for MGP?

Over the course of my rotational internship at MGP, I interned with several different departments within the Company including Corporate Relations, Sales, Customer Service, and Human Resources. My responsibilities varied from department to department and ranged from designing an informational brochure that was used at a tradeshow, to creating an e-blast that was sent to potential customers, to writing news articles for the internal newsletter, to crafting an internal tracking system for credit and debit memos, and conducting phone interview screenings. I was also involved with the implementation of the new Company website.

How have your career and/or skills evolved at MGP?

While interning at MGP, I participated in projects that gave me added insight that can’t be learned from a textbook. I advanced my skill sets from knowledge to actual applications, which provided me the opportunity to grow. My mentors supplied me with experiences that offered valuable information that can be used in my future career.

Why did you choose employment at MGP?

I was looking to complete my internship at a company where I would gain exposure to value-added projects that would provide me with core business skills that I would be able to utilize in my future career endeavors. I wanted to intern at MGP because I felt the company was an innovative company producing ingredients that make a difference.

What do you like best about working at MGP?

I liked the company culture, the people, the projects, but most of all I liked coming to work each day and learning. I had great mentors who constantly provided me with unique projects that offered valuable information. I honesty enjoyed coming to work, which was a great feeling!

What are the most satisfying aspects of your role at MGP?

The most satisfying aspects of my role at MGP were the diverse opportunities to learn key business fundamentals, the relaxed corporate culture, and the interactions with my superiors.

How have MGP’s values been reflected in your work?

I feel MGP’s three-part promise to provide support, ingenuity, and performance to its customers was present in my work. I feel I took my mentors’ knowledge of MGP and utilized this information in the work that I performed for the company. This gave me the opportunity to develop my personal work and skill sets within the business world.

Describe the most significant or exciting challenge you have had while working for MGP.

I feel the most significant or exciting challenge I had while interning at MGP, was my involvement in a major industry tradeshow. The event was more sizable than I anticipated, but I was able to set aside the feeling of being overwhelmed and enjoy the show and the hands-on learning it provided. It was truly a wonderful and exciting experience.
